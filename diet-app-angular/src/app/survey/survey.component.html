Information for people who are to have a body composition test (body composition analysis) What is a BIA test? How to prepare for the test? The BIA test measures impedance (that is, a type of electrical resistance composed of resistance and reactance) of tissues, using a set of surface electrodes connected to a computer analyzer and using a current of a given low frequency and intensity. The examination is completely painless. The phenomenon of resistance is related to the specific resistance of individual tissues, while the reactance is mainly due to the electric capacity of cell membranes, which, due to their structure, act as capacitors. Two electrodes are placed around the subject's wrist / forearm and two more around the ankle. The measurement is performed under the following conditions: • During the examination, you should undress to your underwear • The test is performed lying down What the study evaluates: The following parameters: cellular body mass (metabolic age), muscle mass, fat mass, total body water, intercellular and intracellular water and lean body mass, and estimate basal metabolism. How to prepare for the test? 1. Please don't drink any liquids or water for 2-3 hours before the test 2. Please don't eat for 2-3 hours before the test 3. Please do not drink coffee or alcohol at least 24 hours, preferably 48 hours before the examination 4. The last strenuous physical effort should be completed at least 6 hours before the test, it is permissible
